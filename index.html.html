💡 Pro Tips
⚡ Check ZMS before leaving the van ▼
Opening ZMS takes 2 minutes and shapes your entire approach. Check: Is the ONT online? What alarms are active? How many ONTs on the same PON port are affected? This tells you whether you're at the right address before you knock.
🔌 Green = APC = correct for KCOM GPON ▼
Never mate a blue (UPC) patchlead to a green (APC) port. The 8° angle mismatch adds 3–8 dB of silent loss. Symptoms look identical to a fibre fault and cleaning does nothing. Always check connector colour before mating.
⚠️ Neighbours down too? Don't work at this house ▼
In ZMS, check the PON port view. If multiple customers show LOSi or offline simultaneously, the fault is upstream of the splitter. Working at one premises won't fix it. Escalate to NOC immediately.
📊 BIP errors rising but power looks fine = dirty connector ▼
Rx power at -23 dBm can look fine while BIP errors climb rapidly. The connector corrupts the signal without dropping power below threshold. Check ZMS → Statistics → GTC. Clean the connector — errors should drop to zero within minutes.
🌡️ DOWi alarm = check ONT temperature first ▼
Drift of Window is almost always caused by overheating. An ONT in a hot cupboard will drift out of its upstream time slot, eventually becoming a rogue. Check ZMS temperature reading first.
🔋 DGi with no power complaint = failing PSU adapter ▼
If ZMS shows a DGi but the customer says they never lost power, the PSU adapter is almost certainly failing under load. Swap the PSU before any other investigation — it's the cheapest and quickest fix.
🔋 Power cycle LAST — not first ▼
Power cycling clears the current alarm state and resets counters. Always capture ZMS alarm state, optical power, and BIP counters BEFORE rebooting. The data before your intervention often reveals the cause more clearly than after.
👥 5 questions to ask the customer on arrival ▼
1. When exactly did it stop? (correlate with ZMS timestamp) 2. Sudden stop or gradual? (event vs degradation) 3. Anything changed recently? (building work, furniture moved) 4. Does it happen in rain or at certain times? (environmental) 5. Has anyone touched the white box or the cable? (connector disturbed)
